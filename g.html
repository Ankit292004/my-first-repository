Spaghetti Carbonara

Spaghetti Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish that originated in Rome. It's made with simple ingredients such as eggs, cheese, pancetta, and black pepper. The result is a creamy and flavorful pasta dish that's quick and easy to prepare.

Ingredients:

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or guanciale
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Black pepper to taste
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Cook the spaghetti in a large pot of boiling salted water until al dente.
  2. In a separate pan, saut√© the pancetta until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and black pepper.
  4. Drain the spaghetti and add it to the pan with pancetta, tossing to coat.
  5. Remove the pan from heat and quickly stir in the egg and cheese mixture.
  6. Season with additional black pepper and salt if needed.
  7. Serve immediately and enjoy!
